Things to remember when reviewing umpire reports
1. TrackMan tracks the center of the ball. The data points on the charts represent our best approximation of a baseball relative to the size of the strike zone.
2. Confusion Matrix (insert image of confusion matrix)
- True Strike: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a strike and it is really a strike.
- True Ball: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a ball and it is really a ball.
- False Strike: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a strike, but it's actually a ball. (This is a type I error for you stats nerds.)
- False Ball: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a ball, but it's actually a strike. (Yes this is a type II error you nerd.)
 3. Umpire score is the percent of correct (as determined by TrackMan) calls by the umpire.
