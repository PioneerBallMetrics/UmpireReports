Things to remember when reviewing umpire reports
1. TrackMan tracks the center of the ball. The data points on the charts represent our best approximation of a baseball relative to the size of the strike zone. Please trust your TrackMan operator-give them the benefit of the doubt.
2. Confusion Matrix
- True Strike: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a strike and it is really a strike.
- True Ball: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a ball and it is really a ball.
- False Strike: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a strike, but it's actually a ball.
- False Ball: These are the pitches where the umpire calls a ball, but it's actually a strike.
3. Umpire score is the percent of correct (as determined by TrackMan) calls by the umpire.
4. TrackMan files have to go through TrackMan and be verified before it is recevied. Due to the delay Umpire Reports may take a few days to develop.
5. If challenge pitches are not on the reports then the data from that game in regards to challenges have not been submitted yet to be uploaded.
